Research and Projects

Abstract


In this paper we discuss the structure and implementations of the planar graph-coloring problem (PGCP). We briefly look at well-known classical algorithms used to solve the PGCP, but primarily focus on the quantum computational angle. Grover’s search is a well-known quantum algorithm that offers a quadratic advantage relative to its classical counterparts. We inspect its application to the PGCP and build a corresponding quantum circuit.

Abstract


Black holes seem like they have no entropy but they possess the maximum entropy of the universe. In this paper, we will be discussing how blackholes contradict the fundamentals of quantum mechanics as well as thermodynamics and the necessity of the theory to describe the entropy of blackholes. In this paper we will further discuss what parameters the entropy of black hole depends on and precisely derive the equation that calculates the entropy of the blackhole

Project Description


Our project involved the creation of a custom gym environment tailored for Baghchal, an ancient and traditional Nepali board game. Baghchal, rooted in Nepali culture, is a strategic two-player game that dates back centuries, offering a rich gaming experience. Our focus was on developing an environment conducive to training agents using the Stablebaseline DQN algorithm.

Project Description


Data Nexus, a free easy to use platform, is designed taking into consideration the arduous process of accessing data about space. Data Nexus simplifies data access across diverse categories, including space, climate, and more. It streamlines data retrieval with 1500 categorized datasets and offers accurate future climate predictions based on historical CO2 emissions.
